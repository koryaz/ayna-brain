# Summary

Emollients are ingredients that soften, smooth, and improve the feel of the skin surface.

They help reduce roughness and improve skin texture by filling gaps between surface skin cells.

Emollients are a core component of most moisturizers.

# Scientific Background

Emollients improve the physical condition of the stratum corneum and contribute to skin softness and flexibility.

Many emollients also contribute to barrier support and improved cosmetic elegance.

# Common Examples

* Squalane
* Caprylic/Capric Triglyceride
* Various plant oils
* Fatty alcohols
* Some silicones

# Why It Matters

Emollients may help:

* Improve skin softness
* Improve skin smoothness
* Reduce rough texture
* Improve moisturizer performance

# Routine Implications

Most effective moisturizers contain a combination of:

* Humectants
* Emollients
* Occlusives

Emollients are often important for user comfort and product acceptance.

# Safety Notes

Most emollients have low irritation potential.

Individual tolerance may vary.

# Common Misconceptions

## "All oils are occlusives."

False.

Many oils function primarily as emollients.

## "Emollients and humectants are the same."

False.

They support skin hydration through different mechanisms.

# Decision Rules

When skin roughness, discomfort, or texture concerns are present, AYNA should consider products containing appropriate emollient systems.
