# Summary

Trans-Epidermal Water Loss (TEWL) is the passive movement and evaporation of water from the skin into the external environment.

TEWL is a normal biological process. However, excessive TEWL is commonly associated with impaired skin barrier function, dehydration, irritation, and increased skin sensitivity.

TEWL is one of the most important concepts in cosmetic skincare because many hydration, barrier repair, and moisturizer recommendations are ultimately aimed at reducing excessive water loss.

# Scientific Background

Water continuously diffuses from deeper skin layers toward the skin surface and evaporates into the environment.

The stratum corneum and its lipid matrix regulate this process.

When barrier integrity is reduced, TEWL typically increases.

Factors that may increase TEWL include:

* Barrier damage
* Excessive exfoliation
* Harsh cleansing
* Environmental stressors
* Low humidity environments

# Why It Matters

Excessive TEWL may contribute to:

* Dehydration
* Tightness
* Flaking
* Increased sensitivity
* Reduced tolerance to active ingredients

Many users describe these symptoms as "dry skin" even when the primary issue is excessive water loss.

# Routine Implications

To help manage excessive TEWL:

* Support barrier function
* Use appropriate moisturizers
* Avoid unnecessary irritation
* Introduce strong actives gradually

Ingredient classes that may help:

* Humectants
* Emollients
* Occlusives

# Safety Notes

TEWL itself is not a diagnosis.

Persistent symptoms should be evaluated within the broader context of skin barrier health and user-reported symptoms.

# Common Misconceptions

## "TEWL is always bad."

False.

Some TEWL is normal and necessary.

## "All moisturizers work the same way."

False.

Different ingredient classes reduce TEWL through different mechanisms.

# Decision Rules

If a user reports:

* Tightness
* Flaking
* Increased sensitivity
* Dehydration symptoms

AYNA should evaluate possible barrier dysfunction and elevated TEWL before recommending additional active ingredients.
