# Summary

Petrolatum is a highly effective occlusive ingredient used to reduce transepidermal water loss (TEWL) and support skin barrier function.

It is one of the most extensively studied moisturizing ingredients in dermatology and cosmetic science.

Despite its simplicity and low cost, petrolatum has strong evidence supporting its effectiveness in managing dryness and supporting compromised skin barriers.

# Scientific Background

Petrolatum forms a protective layer on the skin surface that significantly reduces water evaporation.

It does not add water to the skin directly.

Instead, it helps retain existing moisture by limiting TEWL.

Petrolatum is commonly used in moisturizers, healing ointments, and barrier-repair products.

# Routine Implications

Petrolatum is commonly used as the final step of a moisturizing routine.

It is frequently combined with:

* Humectants
* Emollients
* Barrier Repair Ingredients

Petrolatum helps retain moisture but does not replace hydration-focused ingredients.

# Safety Notes

Petrolatum has a very low risk of irritation or allergy when properly refined for cosmetic use.

AYNA should not classify petrolatum as inherently harmful, pore-clogging, or unsafe based on common internet myths.

# Common Misconceptions

## "Petrolatum is unsafe because it comes from petroleum."

False.

Cosmetic-grade petrolatum is highly refined and widely used in dermatology.

## "Petrolatum moisturizes the skin by adding water."

False.

Petrolatum primarily reduces water loss.

## "Petrolatum prevents skin from breathing."

False.

It reduces TEWL but does not completely seal the skin.

# Decision Rules

* Significant dryness
