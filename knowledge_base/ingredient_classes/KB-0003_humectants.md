# Summary

Humectants are ingredients that attract and retain water within the outer layers of the skin.

They are one of the primary mechanisms used in cosmetic moisturization.

Humectants help improve skin hydration and may reduce feelings of tightness associated with dehydration.

# Scientific Background

Humectants bind water molecules and increase water content in the stratum corneum.

Their effectiveness depends on overall formulation design and skin barrier condition.

Humectants are often combined with emollients and occlusives.

# Common Examples

* Glycerin
* Hyaluronic Acid
* Urea
* Betaine
* Panthenol
* Sodium PCA

# Why It Matters

Humectants may help:

* Improve hydration
* Reduce dehydration symptoms
* Improve skin comfort
* Support overall moisturizer performance

# Routine Implications

Humectants are commonly found in:

* Serums
* Moisturizers
* Masks

Humectants alone may not be sufficient for users with significant barrier dysfunction.

# Safety Notes

Most common humectants have low irritation potential.

Tolerance should be evaluated at the full formula level rather than by ingredient class alone.

# Common Misconceptions

## "Humectants are moisturizers."

Partially true.

Humectants are one component of many moisturizing systems.

## "Humectants alone solve dehydration."

Not always.

Barrier support and TEWL reduction may also be necessary.

# Decision Rules

If dehydration is suspected:

AYNA should prioritize products containing effective humectants together with appropriate barrier-support strategies.
