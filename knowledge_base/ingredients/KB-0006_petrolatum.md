# Summary

Petrolatum is a highly effective occlusive ingredient used to reduce transepidermal water loss (TEWL) and support skin barrier function.

It is one of the most extensively studied moisturizing ingredients in dermatology and cosmetic science.

# Scientific Background

Petrolatum forms a protective layer on the skin surface that significantly reduces water evaporation.

It does not add water to the skin directly.

Instead, it helps retain existing moisture by limiting TEWL.

# Common Misconceptions

## "Petrolatum is unsafe because it comes from petroleum."

False.

Cosmetic-grade petrolatum is highly refined and widely used in dermatology.

## "Petrolatum moisturizes the skin by adding water."

False.

Petrolatum primarily reduces water loss.
