# KB-0001 Skin Barrier

Status: Approved  
Category: Skin Biology

Core ideas:

- Skin barrier is a protective interface.
- Stratum corneum can be understood using the brick-and-mortar model.
- Barrier integrity affects hydration, sensitivity, irritation, and product tolerance.
- Main lipid groups in the barrier include ceramides, cholesterol, and free fatty acids.
- Barrier damage should change AYNA routine recommendations.

---

# KB-0002 TEWL

Status: Approved  
Category: Skin Biology

Core ideas:

- TEWL means transepidermal water loss.
- Some water loss is normal.
- Excessive TEWL may indicate impaired barrier function.
- Occlusives help reduce TEWL.
- Humectants help attract and retain water but do not replace barrier support.

---

# KB-0003 Humectants

Status: Approved  
Category: Ingredient Classes

Core ideas:

- Humectants attract and retain water.
- Examples include glycerin, hyaluronic acid, urea, betaine, panthenol, and sodium PCA.
- Humectants often work best with emollients and occlusives.

---

# KB-0004 Emollients

Status: Approved  
Category: Ingredient Classes

Core ideas:

- Emollients soften and smooth the skin surface.
- They improve cosmetic feel and skin comfort.
- Examples include squalane, caprylic/capric triglyceride, plant oils, fatty alcohols, and some silicones.

---

# KB-0005 Occlusives

Status: Approved  
Category: Ingredient Classes

Core ideas:

- Occlusives reduce TEWL by forming a protective layer on the skin surface.
- Examples include petrolatum, mineral oil, dimethicone, lanolin, and beeswax.

---

# KB-0006 Petrolatum

Status: Approved  
Category: Ingredients

Core ideas:

- Petrolatum is a highly effective occlusive.
- It reduces TEWL and supports barrier recovery.
- It does not directly add water to the skin.
- It has very low irritation risk when cosmetic-grade and properly refined.

# Sensitive Skin

Status: In Progress

Core ideas already established:

- Sensitive skin is not a skin type.
- Sensitive skin is better understood as a tendency toward increased reactivity and reduced product tolerance.
- Sensitive skin should be modeled as a trait, not simply as a temporary state.
- Sensitive skin is not the same as barrier damage.
- Sensitive skin is not the same as redness-prone skin.
- Sensitive skin is not the same as allergic reaction.

Important distinction:

```text
sensitivity_prone = long-term tendency / trait
barrier_damage = current condition / state
```

# Redness-Prone Skin

Status: In Progress

Core ideas already established:

- Not all redness means sensitive skin.
- A user may flush after wine, heat, hot showers, emotions, or stress while still tolerating cosmetic products well.
- Redness-prone tendency should be modeled separately from sensitivity-prone tendency.
- Visible vessels may indicate vascular reactivity or rosacea-like features but should not be diagnosed by AYNA.

---

# Rosacea vs Sensitive Skin

Status: In Progress

Core ideas already established:

- Rosacea is a chronic skin disease and outside AYNA's diagnostic scope.
- AYNA must not diagnose rosacea.
- AYNA may identify rosacea-like symptoms or vascular reactivity and recommend dermatologist evaluation if appropriate.
- Triggers may include heat, alcohol, sun, stress, hot drinks, spicy food, and hot showers.
- Couperose is often used cosmetically to describe visible superficial vessels, but it is not the same as a formal diagnosis.

---

# Barrier Damage vs Sensitive Skin

Status: In Progress

Core ideas already established:

- Barrier damage is a current state.
- Sensitive-prone skin is a trait.
- Users may confuse temporary barrier damage with sensitive skin.
- Baseline history is important.
- AYNA should compare baseline vs current state before classifying the user as sensitivity-prone.

Important reasoning pattern:

```text
Before assuming sensitive skin:
compare baseline behavior with current symptoms.
```

---

# Irritation vs Allergy

Status: In Progress

Core ideas already established:

- Burning, stinging, dryness, flaking, and tightness often suggest irritation.
- Itching, rash, swelling, and delayed reaction after a new product may suggest allergic reaction.
- AYNA must use probabilistic language.
- AYNA should not diagnose allergy.
- Swelling, especially around eyes or lips, increases safety concern.

# Fragrance & Allergens

Status: Introduced / In Progress

Core ideas already established:

- Fragrance / Parfum / Perfume may represent a mixture of many aromatic compounds.
- Fragrance presence increases allergy risk and sensitivity risk, especially for sensitivity-prone users.
- Essential oils are not automatically safer because they are natural.
- Fragrance is not automatically “bad,” but it is a meaningful risk flag.

Important product logic:

```text
fragrance_present ≠ bad_product
fragrance_present = increased_risk_for_some_users
```
